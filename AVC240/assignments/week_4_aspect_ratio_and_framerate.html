Project Objectives: Students will demonstrate knowledge of framerate and aspect ratio by constructing a series of shots.

Course Objectives:

  • Stage a scene for a character and/or product shot.
  • Apply the appropriate lines of action to virtual cameras in various environments.
  • Compare and contrast the visualization process in traditional arts vs. directing a shot in a
    digital environment.
  • Manipulate aperture, depth of field, lenses, and attributes needed to control a virtual
    camera in various environments.

Project:

Edit a showcase video in AE with the following elements. 

  • New Title card with subtle scale movement
  • Aspect Ratio: Soccer Scene: model the goal and construct a background that uses a material you imported from polyhaven. Be sure to use linear keyframes on the camera
    • 4:3,
    • 16;9
    • 2.38:1
  • Aspect Ratio: A scene of your own design that uses a Mixamo character, a camera with a 24 mm focal length, and a spot light.
    • 4:3,
    • 16;9
    • 2.38:1
  • Framerate: a bouncing ball that exhibits excellent squash and stretch in three different framerates
    • 24 fps
    • 12 fps
    • 8 fps
  • Framerate Cartoon: A cartoon rendered bouncing ball with exaggerated squash and stretch in three different framerates.
    • 24 fps
    • 12 fps
    • 8 fps
  • Framerate Character: two Mixamo characters using jump animation.
    • Cartoon shading, on-the-threes, exaggerated jumping squash and stretch
    • Standard shading, on-the-ones, standard jump animation

Project Objective: Students will create storyboards and style frames for an animated title sequence for a film, show, or game.

  • Course Competencies
    • Compare and contrast the visualization process in traditional arts vs. directing a shot in a
      digital environment. (I)
    • Manipulate aperture, depth of field, lenses, and attributes needed to control a virtual
      camera in various environments. (I-II)
    • Create conceptual sketches, plans and storyboards for a project. (III)
    • Apply lighting and compositional concepts to create a mood in a digital environment. (IV)
    • Determine appropriate camera attributes and shot flow using plans and storyboards. (IV-V)
    • Apply shot flow and directing techniques to a project. (V)
    • Stage a scene for a character and/or product shot. (VI)

Project Requirements:

  • Design Brief Requirements
    1. The MTVG (Movie, TV show, Game), Why did you select this MTVG? Answer this questions in at least 200 words. 
      • The purpose of this project is NOT to reproduce the existing title sequence, but rather reimagine the title sequence in an original manner.
    2. 10 credits to use in the title sequence (director, actors, game designers, etc)  
    3. Selected Font - google fonts, https://www.dafont.com/ - please provide a visual example of the font
    4. Storyboard - use one of the previously provided storyboard templates, you must include the following
      • Quick sketches,
      • lensing,
      • camera techniques,
      • shot size,
      • shot framing
      • camera movements
    • Music - do not use the existing sound track for the existing title sequence, select a new sound track
    • Style References: Exploring the "art of the title" select four specific frames. Visual trait/style want to emulate in your sequence. DO NOT use any of title sequences I selected, explore and find your own references.
      • Reference 1: What traits from this sequence do you want to use in your Title Sequence?
      • Reference 2: What traits from this sequence do you want to use in your Title Sequence?
      • Reference 3: What traits from this sequence do you want to use in your Title Sequence?
      • Reference 4: What traits from this sequence do you want to use in your Title Sequence?
    • Style Frames - 
      • Suggested process
        • Create a new .blend file for each board in your storyboards and try previsualize the final rendered title sequence as best as possible.

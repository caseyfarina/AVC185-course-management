Project Objectives: Students will learn how motion blur is created by adjusting shutter speed. Students will present their work in a showcase reel.

Course Objectives:

  • Stage a scene for a character and/or product shot.
  • Apply the appropriate lines of action to virtual cameras in various environments.
  • Compare and contrast the visualization process in traditional arts vs. directing a shot in a
    digital environment.
  • Manipulate aperture, depth of field, lenses, and attributes needed to control a virtual
    camera in various environments.

Project:

Edit a showcase video in AE with the following elements. Each element should be three seconds.

  • Title card with subtle scale movement
  • focal length shots
    • 24 mm
    • 50 mm
    • 120 mm
  • DOF with 3D objects - use lights and material emission to enhance the Bokeh 
    • foreground in focus
    • midground in focus
    • background in focus
  • Shutter Speed and Motion Blur shots - construct an animated spinning object as the subject
    • Fast shutter (minimal motion blur)
    • medium shutter (medium motion blur)
    • Slow shutter (pronounced motion blur)
    • Various levels of blur - vary the speed at which the objects are spinning
    • Motion blur Movie - render a three second video of the spinning objects with motion blur
    • "Flash Effect" - use mixamo to create a running figure and render a still of the running figure that communicates speed through motion blur

    • A presentation video discussing the following (use OBS to display the showcase during your discussion)

      • What was challenging?
      • How does shutter speed effect motion blur in an image?

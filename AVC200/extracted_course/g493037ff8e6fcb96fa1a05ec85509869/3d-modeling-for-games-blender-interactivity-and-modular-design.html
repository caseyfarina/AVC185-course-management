Project Objective: Students will apply 3D modeling skills to create a series of modular props for a game level.

Course Competencies Covered:

  • Use principles of animation (IV)
  • Create non-linear experiences (V)
  • Create and use advanced buttons for user input (VI)
  • Create movie clips, navigate timelines (VII)
  • Manipulate text and related objects, variables and expressions (VIII)
  • Use sound in production (IX)

Project Requirements: Use Blender to model some modular wall, pillar, and prop assets. Block out the layout of your level by replacing all of the wall, corner, and pillar assets from the Unity packages with your own assets. Consider how the wall, pillar, and door design will work together. How will they share a similar design language. Don't apply any materials to the assets you designed in Blender. We'll address some of the material issues next week. Convert each asset into a prefab before placing them into the level so you can easily update their appearance later. 

  • 2 different wall designs
  • 3 different pillar designs
  • 2 different door/opening designs
  • 1 cup from the tutorial
  • 1 additional small prop of your own design (work within your skills)

Revisions:

  • Implement any changes and suggestions from your feedback.
  • Adjust/redo the keyframes for your bouncing ball and fruit. Pay particular attention to the timing of the ball movement and how it relates to the scale deformations.
  • Incorporate your small props into the world design
  • Revise your reveal room to include the following
    • Turn the music off when the reveal sound is triggered. 
    • Disable the reveal collider object at the end of the reveal Unity Event sequence to ensure the reveal does not double trigger. 
    • Add a second level of animation to the reveal

Additions:

  • Design an entrance area from your Blender assets. This area should lead into the other three rooms/areas and feature some form of interactivity. 

Deliverable:

  • A presentation video (less than 3 minutes) detailing:
    • How you constructed each of the three rooms
    • Demonstration of the interactive functionality in each room
    • What challenges you faced and how you overcame them
    • Your approach to animating the bouncing balls and movable objects
    • How you implemented the animated reveal in the third room
    • Any questions or feedback

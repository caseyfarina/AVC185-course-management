Third Person 3D Game: Second Draft
Requirements:

  • Character:
    • Be sure you have added the new rigidBody controller package. 
      • thirdPersonWithBounce.unitypackage -drag the package into your assets folder
        • Please install the free version of DoTween, This packaged is reference by the scripts. 
        • https://assetstore.unity.com/packages/tools/animation/dotween-hotween-v2-27676
        • It will appear in the "My Assets" section of the package manager
    • Model a custom mesh for your main character, rig and animate it in Cinema 4D
      • thirdPersonAnimationController.cs Your character should have the following animations
        • Run
        • Idle
        • Jump
  • Obstacles
    • 8 different moving obstacles
      • I would like to see some more of your own creativity. How could you expand the ideas we explored in class?
      • Remember to place the _bumpPlayer.cs script on the objects that should propel the player backwards. Take the time to tune the strength of this script appropriately for the game play
    • 3 different moving platforms
  • Environment
    • 8 different environment objects that are repeated throughout the level that enrich the design of the world.
    • 4 different animated environment objects that are repeated throughout the level that enrich the design of the world. These objects could be animated in C4D or Unity.
    • Consider taking advantage of scale in your design. How could large objects occupy the 3D space above and below the level?
    • Are you still having trouble with your text? Please do not use the Bevel object on the text. Use the caps rounding and re-import into Unity.
    • Include at least two moving platforms. movingPlatform.cs 
  • Particles
    • 1 environmental particle effect. Something like snow or sparkles that enhances a section of the level
    • 2 particle effects that are attached to game objects. Some Possibilities
      • swinging fire balls
      • energy trails on projectiles
      • sparkles surrounding certain objects
  • Music
    • Music that fits the energy level and theme of the game.
    • A sound effect for the character jump
    • A sound effect for the character bump
  • Build
    • Build the project for WebGL and upload to itch. If you're on windows then you can build a zip version of the game, that is not an option if you are on a Mac computer.

Deliverable:

  • A link to the game on itch.io
  • post the link to slack 
  • play test all the other games posted to slack and provide the following feedback
    • how many times you played the game
    • Is the game too hard or too easy? How should the difficulty be adjusted? What changes could be implemented.
    • A strong component of the visual design
    • A weak component of the visual design that could be improved.

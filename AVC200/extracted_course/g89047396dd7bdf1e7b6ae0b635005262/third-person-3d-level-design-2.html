Project Objective: Complete a level design in Unity. Model and paint high detail assets for the game

Course Objectives;

    • Navigate Program Interface and tools.
    • Create storyboards, develop concepts, visual style
    • Manipulate text and related objects, variables and expressions

Project

  • Build your game assets and lay them out in Unity.  ( >= "means greater than or equal to")
    • Requirements
      • >= 3 High Detail Game  assets
        • These objects should be modeled, UV unwrapped, and painted in Substance painter
        • Make sure these objects are modular and reusable
        • Consider which objects will be used throughout your design. These objects would benefit from a higher level of detail
        • the previously designed platform, cylinder, and sphere do NOT count as your high detail objects
      • >= 5 materials that combine your color palette with texture/pattern palette
        • These materials should be designed in substance painter on the default plane. This will assure that they can be applied to a variety of different meshes
      • Consider modular design
        • Model parts in C4D
        • Assemble the parts in Unity
        • Use Illustrator for mechanical parts extruded from an intricate profile
        • build in a modular fashion, use prefabs
      • Do NOT model the main character, we will be working on that in the coming weeks
    • Level Requirements
      • >= 5 different zones
        • Each zone should have at least one animating or physics based obstacle.
        • Use prefabs and parent game objects to organize your level design in the editor
        • Use cloner scripts at least once to layout obstacles in an intentional pattern
    • World Building
      • What will the player see just outside the limits of the level?
      • How can you add background objects to make it feel like a complete world?
      • How do they populate the world in Fall Guys to give the impression of a world beyond the level?
        • https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=s8uYE9r3hIg
        • https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FcITAzKW3fY
        • take note of how a former student populated the world using scale.

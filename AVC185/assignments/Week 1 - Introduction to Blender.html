Project Objectives

Students will become familiar with 3D modeling interface and core workflow by manipulating primitives, configuring cameras, applying materials, and rendering a movie file.

Project

Create an https://caseyfarina.github.io/lecture-redirects/?class=avc185&lecture=week1-lecture1(not representational) scene in Blender that uses the following 

  • At least 30 3D primitives. Use at least one of each of the following
    • UV Sphere
    • Cylinder
    • Cone
    • Ico Sphere
    • Torus
  • At least three point lights of various intensities
  • Place camera at the ideal location to view the scene
  • Use the following tools during the scene's construction
    • Move
    • Rotate
    • Scale
    • shift + transform (nudge)
    • ctrl + transform (snap)
  • Enable smooth shading on the objects you would like to appear smooth
  • Properly manage the topology so the primitives have an appropriate density of faces/polygons
  • Use orthographic projections to precisely place objects in space
  • Apply materials to the objects to create a compelling abstract image
    • You may use materials that you configure 
    • or simple material from Blender Kit

Course Competencies

    • Apply basic geometry principles for construction of 3-D models
    • Execute finished models by employing materials, environmental effects, and rendering
    • Apply basic animation principles to models.
